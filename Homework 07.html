Question 6

The Central Limit Theorem explains that when your sample size increases, the distribution of averages become more normal (bell shape curve become taller/narrower). It is essential that your sample size is as large as possible because the more data you collect, the more accurate your distribution is to the actual population proportion. This would result in having a more precise distribution (a taller bell/shape curve).
